Workshops


Learn from industry leaders

  • 6:30 - 7:00: An Introduction to the Hackathon Experience

    • Hosted by Daniel Wei, founder of TeenHacksHTX.

    What is a hackathon? How do you compete? What are some strategies for winning? The 5th place international winner of CovLab Global and Silicon Valley Hacks introduces our first-time hackathon attendees to the hackathon.

  • 7:00 - 7:30: Build a Cloud-Connected AR/VR App in 15 Minutes or Less

    • Hosted by Alon Grinshpoon, echoAR
    Topic Areas: Spatial Computing, Augmented Reality, Virtual Reality, Cloud, Enterprise, Dev Tools

    The workshop will show how to quickly create AR/VR apps with no technical skills or coding required and scale existing apps by connecting them to the cloud. Workshop participants will learn how to create real-time cloud-connected augmented and virtual reality (AR/VR) apps using the echoAR platform. Participants are encouraged to bring a laptop and an Android phone, or just follow along the live demonstration. Platforms for experimentation include: Google ARCore, WebXR, Vuforia, Unity-based apps, and more.

  • 7:30 - 8:00: An Introduction to Deep Learning and Neural Networks

    • Hosted by Ujjaini Das, Obra D. Tompkins High School.
    Topic Areas: Machine Learning, Neural Networks, Multivariable Calculus, NumPy/SciPy.

    This workshop will provide an in-depth, mathematical introduction to one of the most fascinating supervised learning tools: Neural Networks! Many libraries such as TensorFlow and Scikit Learn ease the process of creating neural networks. However, in this workshop, I hope to give you an understanding of the internal workings of the structure and the math involved. At the end, I will run you through creating one of your first, basic neural networks. No coding experience or calculus knowledge is required!

  • 8:00 - 9:00: An Introduction to DevOps

    • Hosted by Mason Egger from DigitalOcean, our Platinum Level sponsor.

  • 9:00 - 10:00: Startup Tips for Student Founders

    • Hosted by Tyler Marx, CEO of Belacam

  • Plus you'll have mentors willing to help out throughout the event!


F.A.Q

  • What is a hackathon?

    It's a place where programmers, engineers, designers, and entrepreneurs come together to build an awesome project in a short amount of time. Get ready to learn, build something cool, and meet new people, while competing for prizes.

  • What if I’m a beginner?

    TeenHacksHTX encourages students of all levels to come! In addition, we will be conducting workshops during the event, and we'll always have a team member available to help you.

  • Who can attend?

    All high school students are welcome to attend, from across the world - no matter your location. If you are older or younger and still want to participate, shoot us an email.

  • Does it cost anything?

    TeenHacksHTX Spring 2020 is completely free thanks to donations from our generous sponsors. If you appreciate the work we do, however, we do take personal donations!

  • What if I don't have a team?

    In the beginning, we’ll have a team formation session to make sure you’re not alone. You can have teams as large or small as you want, but only teams of 2-4 will be eligible for prizes. We do recommend you to come with a team in mind already however - it'll save you time and effort - so encourage your friends and peers to register as well!

  • What kind of swag will I receive?

    Unfortunately, due to COVID-19 shipping costs, we won't be able to send out swag during this event. However, winners will still have their prizes mailed to them.

  • Can I begin working before the event starts?

    Absolutely not! You must begin coding within the 24-hour allotted time. If we find out you started early, you could be tiered, or even disqualified.

  • 24 hours is kind of long - do I have to work the whole time?

    Of course not! Besides taking breaks to eat and sleep, we'll also be conducting minigames and challenges periodically.

  • Are there any restrictions on what type of language can be used or objectives achieved?

    Absolutely not. You're free to code whatever kind of software you can.
